Dhorpatan Hunting Reserve

The Dhorpatan hunting reserve beckons every traveler with its lofty hills, terraced fields, and solitary trails. However, this beauty remains relatively undiscovered by many. As the sole hunting reserve in Nepal catering to enthusiasts of hunting sports, Dhorpatan not only provides an exciting hunting experience but also boasts a stunning landscape featuring lush forests, expansive marshes, and sprawling meadows. It serves as a captivating destination to reconnect with Mother Nature, ensuring a fulfilling and enriching journey.

Rupa Taal

Pokhara is renowned for its breathtaking lakes and mountains, creating an atmosphere of tranquility. Among the city's well-known lakes like Fewa Lake and Begnas Taal, there's also the stunning but lesser-known Rupa Taal. As the third-largest lake in the area, Rupa Taal remains tucked away from the bustling central city. Encircled by lush green hills and forests, the lake provides a peaceful haven for those seeking quality time away from the crowds of pilgrims. Despite being part of a vibrant city, Rupa Taal exudes a sense of isolation, transporting visitors to a distant world. The clear lake, reflecting the beauty of its surroundings, imparts a soothing effect, inviting people to sit for hours in contemplation, introspection, and rejuvenation. This hidden gem in Nepal serves as an energy booster and is a must-visit while exploring Pokhara.

Bardiya National Park

Nestled along the southwest border of Nepal, adjacent to India, Bardiya National Park has a rich history. Originally designated as the Karnali Wildlife Reserve in 1976, it underwent a significant transformation in 1982, acquiring the name Bardiya Wildlife Reserve and expanding its boundaries. Subsequently, in 1988, the area earned the prestigious designation of National Park. The primary highlight of this natural haven is the opportunity to spot Bengal tigers, making it a focal point for wildlife enthusiasts. Beyond its renowned Bengal tigers, the park is a habitat for 30 mammal species, including endangered ones like the Bengal Tiger, One-horned Rhinoceros, Wild Elephant, Swamp Deer, and Black Buck. Bardiya National Park also boasts a diverse avian population, with over 230 bird species, featuring Bengal flamingos, Lesser flamingos, and Sarus cranes among its winged residents.

Ilam

Ilam, though officially a district in Nepal, is renowned for its tea gardens, particularly in the small area of Kanyam. However, Ilam offers more than just its tea plantations; it is a district with numerous captivating locations waiting to be explored, making it an exceptional destination for experiencing the essence of Eastern Nepal. Situated in the far eastern part of Nepal, near the border with India, Ilam is approximately 600 kilometers away from Kathmandu, its headquarters. Despite the distance, Ilam is easily accessible from Kathmandu and various parts of Nepal, serving as a gateway to the Mt. Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek. The name "Ilam" is derived from the Limbu language, where 'Il' signifies twisted, and 'Lam' denotes roads. As the capital of Nepal's Tea Garden, Ilam boasts renowned tea known for its quality, taste, and authenticity. Sampling Ilam tea is a must during your trip, as it adds a worthwhile dimension to your exploration of this unique district.
